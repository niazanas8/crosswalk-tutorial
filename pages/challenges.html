Challenges in Crosswalk Detection

Even though crosswalks seem simple, detecting them in real-world scenes is not. Research papers point out several recurring challenges that limit both classical and deep learning approaches [1][7].

Appearance Variability

Different cities and countries use different styles (zebra, ladder, continental). Over time, paint fades or gets resurfaced, making lines harder to see.

Occlusion

Vehicles, cyclists, or groups of people often block the crosswalk partially. This lowers detector confidence and increases false negatives.

Illumination & Weather

Harsh shadows, night scenes, glare from headlights, or rain/snow introduce noise. Classical methods are especially fragile under these conditions.

Viewpoint & Scale

Onboard cameras capture crosswalks at angles and distances that distort the geometry. Stripes may look narrower or curved depending on perspective.

Domain Shift

Models trained on one city or dataset may not work well in another location because of changes in pavement color, marking style, or camera setup.
