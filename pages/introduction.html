Crosswalks provide safe, designated zones for pedestrians to cross streets, yet automatically detecting them is challenging in practice. A robust detector benefits public safety, supports autonomous driving, and enables assistive navigation for blind or low‑vision users. Detection difficulty arises from appearance variation (country‑specific patterns, wear), occlusion (vehicles, crowds), and environmental factors (shadows, glare, night).

Historically, systems relied on visual patterns such as parallel stripes and strong edges. Modern approaches use deep networks (e.g., object detection and segmentation) and, more recently, vision‑language models (VLMs) that combine visual features with textual reasoning for scene understanding and risk assessment [3][4][5].
