Classical Computer Vision Methods

Classical computer vision methods exploit geometric and photometric cues:

  • Edge & Line Detection: Canny + Hough transforms to find parallel stripes and lane‑like structures.
  • Pattern Templates: Detect repeated bright/dark bands consistent with zebra markings.
  • Structure Analysis: Row/column statistics to capture periodicity [1].

Pros: fast, interpretable, low compute. Cons: brittle to wear, occlusion, shadows, and non‑standard designs.

// Pseudocode: simple stripe periodicity idea
edges = canny(image)
lines  = hough(edges)
clusters = find_parallel(lines)
score = periodic_intensity_along(clusters)
if score > threshold: report CROSSWALK
