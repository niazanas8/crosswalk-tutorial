Vision‑Language Models (VLMs) and multimodal LLMs combine visual perception with textual reasoning. Recent work uses GPT‑4V and related models to assess street‑crossing safety with interpretable rationales [3], and to improve context‑aware pedestrian understanding via self‑supervised semantic cues [4]. Surveys outline broader applications in autonomous driving (perception, planning, data generation) [5]. Other frameworks jointly describe a scene and localize risks in traffic video [6].

Key idea: move from detecting paint to understanding the scene (is it safe to cross now?).
  • Strengths: contextual reasoning, zero/few‑shot adaptability, language explanations.
  • Challenges: latency, hallucinations, prompt sensitivity, safety guarantees.
